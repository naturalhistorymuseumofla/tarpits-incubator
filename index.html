REIMAGINE
LA BREA TAR PITS

La Brea Tar Pits are an urban oasis right here in our backyard—local park, beloved community space, and an Ice Age excavation site critical to the world both in the context of scientific research, and the global climate conversation. Reimagine the experience at the Tar Pits, the museum, and the park through engaging storytelling about this extraordinary resource.

The Opportunity

Reimagine La Brea Tar Pits Experience in Hancock Park and the museum through engaging storytelling around the importance of this Ice Age fossil site.

Conduct An International Competition

For architects and creative team of landscape architects, planners and designers.

Organize an Incubator

A unique event for ideating including thought-leaders from multiple disciplines. Finalist teams to attend.



La Brea Tar Pits represents the best and most complete record of life during the ice age. We have a singular opportunity to reimagine a true global and Los Angeles icon. We are the original miracle on the miracle mile.

SPEAKERS

Dr. Lori Bettison-Varga

President and Director, Natural History Museums of Los Angeles County

Dr. Lori Bettison-Varga oversees all aspects of the management and programs at the Natural History Museums of Los Angeles County, including NHM in Exposition Park, La Brea Tar Pits and museum in Hancock Park, and the William S. Hart Museum in Newhall. Under her leadership, the museums have inaugurated major new initiatives in research, exhibitions, community science, and community engagement. She has overseen a progressive strategic planning process, developing goals that chart a clear course for institutional growth and enhancement...: to expand the roles of three institutions as museums “of, for, and with” L.A.; to create a new interdisciplinary model for understanding and connecting to urban nature; and to strengthen and activate the museums’ research and collections to create and communicate new knowledge about our world. Prior to joining NHMLAC, Dr.Bettision-Varga served as president of Scripps College in Claremont, California. She holds a bachelor’s degree in geology from the University of California, Santa Barbara, and a master’s and doctorate from the University of California, Davis, and has been a teacher and scholar, publishing widely on geology and academic pedagogy.

Marty Kaplan

Director of the Norman Lear Center and professor at USC

Marty Kaplan holds the Norman Lear chair in entertainment, media and society at the USC Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism. He served as chief speechwriter to Vice President Walter F. Mondale and deputy campaign manager of his presidential race. He was a vice president of feature film production at Walt Disney Studios as well as a screenwriter and producer. Marty was associate dean of the USC Annenberg School for 10 years, and is the founding director of its Norman Lear Center. ... His areas of research include the use of narrative for science communication; the political coverage on local TV news; the effects on audiences of public health messages in entertainment; and the impact on democratic values and institutions of the attention economy. His print columns have won six First Place prizes from the LA Press Club.

Walter Parkes

Film Producer & Screenwriter,
Chairman & Co-Founder,
Dreamscape Immersive

Walter Parkes is a motion picture producer, writer and the former head of DreamWorks Studios. Films produced or executive produced by Parkes include Gladiator, Minority Report, the Men In Black series, Flight, Catch Me If You Can, The Mask of Zorro, The Kite Runner, Awakenings, Amistad and many others. As a screenwriter, Parkes is largely acknowledged as a pioneer in the development of the "tech-thriller" genre with WarGames, a Best Original Screenplay Oscar nominee, and Sneakers,... starring Robert Redford. Most recently, Parkes is Co-Founder and Chairman of the location-based virtual reality company Dreamscape Immersive.

PANELISTS

Nancy Bennett

Chief Creative Officer,
Two Bit Circus

Nancy Bennett is the Chief Creative Officer at Two Bit Circus, an interdisciplinary team of artists, inventors, engineers, educators and entrepreneurs passionate about reimagining all forms of entertainment. With a background in immersive, commercial, music video, advertising and television creation, she is responsible for Two Bit Circus’ immersive and cinematic work and has expertise in designing and activating location based multi-user experiences using interactive, haptic and motion platforms.... Bennett has been the creative visionary behind VR projects for clients including the NFL, NBA, Samsung, the USOC, Fox and Wasserman. Her VR music video for Interscope recording artist Alex Aiono, “One At a Time” featuring T-Pain has garnered more than 850K views and is the recipient of the 2018 Lumiere award for best music VR experience. She has designed and launched networks, products, founded and run companies and is a strong creative leader. Nancy lives at the nexus of groundbreaking technologies and lucid cinematic storytelling. Her vast career as an auteur spans the mediums of tech entrepreneurship, film, television, music, and advertising. She transforms entertainment experiences with high-tech amusement to inspire people’s curiosity and wonder.

Dr. Shane Campbell-Staton

Assistant Professor of Biology,
UCLA Institute
for Society and Genetics

Shane Campbell-Staton is an evolutionary biologist and assistant professor in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology and the Institute for Society and Genetics at UCLA. He studies physiological and genomic adaptation to anthropogenic pressures in species around the globe. Much of his work focuses on how human activity, from climate change to urbanization, shapes evolution in wild populations. In addition to his research, he also has a passion for science communication.... His most recent project, The Biology of Superheroes Podcast, combines science fiction with modern research to explore the limits of form and function in the natural world.

Dr. John H. Falk

Director of the Institute for Learning Innovation at Oregon State University

Dr. John H. Falk is Director of the Institute for Learning Innovation and Sea Grant Emeritus Professor of Free-Choice Learning at Oregon State University. He is internationally recognized as a leading expert on free-choice learning; the learning that occurs while visiting science centers, museums, zoos, aquariums or ecotourism sites, watching educational television or surfing the Internet for information. Dr. Falk has authored over 200 articles and chapters in the areas of learning, ecology and education,... two-dozen books, and helped to create several nationally important out-of-school educational curricula. His most recent book is Born to Choose: Evolution, Self & Well-Being (2017, Routledge) which synthesizes research from across the biological and social sciences to develop a new model for why and how people (and other living things) make the endless set of choices they do, every second of every day of their lives. His current research focuses on studying the impacts of free-choice learning settings on the public’s understanding of, interest in and engagement with science and understanding why people utilize free-choice learning settings during their leisure time. He also has a keen interest in exploring new ways of thinking about, measuring and supporting science literacy and professional learning. His awards include the NARST: A worldwide organization for improving science teaching and learning through research Distinguished Career Award ( 2016); Oregon State University, University Outreach and Engagement Vice Provost Award for Excellence, Innovation-Partnerships Award (2016); Council of Scientific Society Presidents Award for Educational Research (2013) and American Alliance of Museums John Cotton Dana Award for Leadership (2010). In 2006 the American Association of Museums included Falk in their list of the 100 most influential museum professionals of the past 100 years. Falk earned a joint doctorate in Ecology and Science Education from the University of California, Berkeley.
